CALIFORNIA CONSUMER PRIVACY ACT (CCPA) / CALIFORNIA PRIVACY RIGHTS ACT (CPRA)

Cal. Civ. Code §§ 1798.100-1798.199

===== OVERVIEW =====

CCPA (effective January 2020) and CPRA (effective January 2023) provide California consumers with rights regarding their personal information.

Applies to businesses that:
- Have gross annual revenues over $25 million
- Buy, sell, or share personal information of 100,000+ California consumers or households
- Derive 50%+ of annual revenues from selling or sharing consumers' personal information

===== KEY DEFINITIONS =====

Personal Information: Information that identifies, relates to, describes, is reasonably capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked to a particular consumer or household

Categories of Personal Information:
- Identifiers (name, address, email, IP address, etc.)
- Commercial information (purchase history, tendencies)
- Biometric information
- Internet or network activity
- Geolocation data
- Audio, electronic, visual, or similar information
- Professional or employment-related information
- Education information
- Inferences drawn from above to create profile

Sensitive Personal Information:
- Social security, driver's license, passport numbers
- Account credentials
- Precise geolocation
- Racial or ethnic origin
- Religious or philosophical beliefs
- Union membership
- Genetic data
- Biometric data for identification
- Health information
- Sex life or sexual orientation
- Citizenship or immigration status

Sale: Selling, renting, releasing, disclosing, disseminating, making available, transferring, or communicating personal information for monetary or other valuable consideration

Share: Sharing, renting, releasing, disclosing, disseminating, making available, transferring, or communicating personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising

===== CONSUMER RIGHTS =====

1. RIGHT TO KNOW
Consumers have right to request:
- Categories of personal information collected
- Categories of sources of personal information
- Business or commercial purpose for collecting/selling/sharing
- Categories of third parties to whom information is disclosed
- Specific pieces of personal information collected

Response time: 45 days (extendable by 45 days)

2. RIGHT TO DELETE
Consumers can request deletion of personal information collected from them

Exceptions:
- Complete transaction
- Detect security incidents
- Debug to identify and repair errors
- Exercise free speech or ensure another's exercise of free speech
- Comply with California Electronic Communications Privacy Act
- Engage in research in public interest
- Enable solely internal uses reasonably aligned with consumer expectations
- Comply with legal obligation
- Make other internal and lawful uses reasonably compatible with context

3. RIGHT TO CORRECT
Consumers have right to request correction of inaccurate personal information

4. RIGHT TO OPT-OUT
Consumers have right to opt-out of:
- Sale of personal information
- Sharing of personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising

Businesses must provide "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link

5. RIGHT TO LIMIT USE OF SENSITIVE PERSONAL INFORMATION
Consumers have right to limit use and disclosure of sensitive personal information to:
- Performing services reasonably expected
- Certain specified business purposes

6. RIGHT TO NON-DISCRIMINATION
Businesses cannot discriminate against consumers for exercising their rights:
- Cannot deny goods or services
- Cannot charge different prices or rates
- Cannot provide different level or quality of goods or services

Exception: Businesses may offer financial incentives or charge different prices if reasonably related to value of consumer's data

===== BUSINESS OBLIGATIONS =====

1. PRIVACY POLICY REQUIREMENTS
Must include:
- Categories of personal information collected
- Categories of sources
- Business or commercial purposes for collection
- Categories of third parties with whom information is shared
- Categories of personal information sold or shared
- Categories of sensitive personal information collected
- Length of time each category will be retained (or criteria for determining)
- Consumer rights and how to exercise them
- Contact information for privacy questions

Must update at least annually

2. NOTICE AT COLLECTION
Must inform consumers at or before collection:
- Categories of personal information to be collected
- Purposes for which categories will be used
- If selling or sharing personal information
- Length of time information will be retained (or criteria)
- Link to privacy policy

3. NOTICE OF RIGHT TO OPT-OUT
Must provide clear and conspicuous link titled "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information"

For sensitive personal information: "Limit the Use of My Sensitive Personal Information"

4. VERIFICATION OF REQUESTS
Must verify identity of consumer making request:
- Two-factor verification for account holders
- Match three data points for non-account holders
- Cannot require account creation to submit request
- Use reasonable verification methods commensurate with sensitivity and risk

5. RESPONSE REQUIREMENTS
- Respond within 45 days (extendable by 45 days)
- If unable to verify, explain why
- Provide information free of charge
- For portable data, provide in readily usable format
- Cover 12-month period preceding request

6. AUTHORIZED AGENTS
Must allow consumers to designate authorized agents to make requests on their behalf
- May require written authorization from consumer
- May verify consumer's identity
- May verify agent's authority

7. TRAINING
Must train employees who handle consumer inquiries about privacy practices and consumer rights

8. SERVICE PROVIDERS AND CONTRACTORS
Contracts must:
- Prohibit retention, use, or disclosure for purposes other than performing services
- Prohibit sale or sharing of personal information
- Prohibit combining personal information with other information
- Grant business right to take reasonable and appropriate steps to ensure compliance

===== MINORS' PERSONAL INFORMATION =====

Under 16 Years:
- Cannot sell or share personal information without affirmative authorization (opt-in)
- Under 13: requires parental consent
- 13-15: may provide own consent

Businesses with actual knowledge of collecting information from minors must comply

===== DATA SECURITY =====

Must implement and maintain reasonable security procedures and practices appropriate to nature of personal information

In event of breach, must notify affected California residents

===== RISK ASSESSMENTS =====

CPRA requires annual cybersecurity audits for businesses that process:
- Personal information of 10+ million consumers
- Share information of 100,000+ consumers
- Derive 50%+ revenue from sharing personal information

===== EXEMPTIONS =====

Certain information types exempt:
- Medical information governed by CMIA
- Protected health information under HIPAA
- Clinical trial data
- Personal information collected as part of research subject to federal regulations
- Information covered by GLBA, FCRA, Driver's Privacy Protection Act

B2B communications exempt until January 1, 2023 (now subject to CPRA)

===== ENFORCEMENT =====

California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA):
- Primary enforcement authority
- Can conduct investigations
- Issue regulations

Civil Penalties:
- Up to $2,500 per violation
- Up to $7,500 per intentional violation or violations involving minors

Private Right of Action:
For data breaches involving specific personal information:
- $100-$750 per consumer per incident
- Or actual damages, whichever is greater
- Requires 30-day notice to business to cure

===== BEST PRACTICES =====

1. DATA MAPPING
- Inventory all personal information collected
- Document sources, purposes, recipients
- Track data flows through organization

2. REQUEST MANAGEMENT
- Automated request intake system
- Centralized tracking
- Clear response workflows
- Audit trail of actions taken

3. VENDOR MANAGEMENT
- Vet vendors for CCPA compliance
- Update contracts with required terms
- Monitor vendor practices
- Maintain vendor inventory

4. TECHNICAL CONTROLS
- Implement data minimization
- Use encryption
- Access controls based on need
- Data retention schedules
- Secure deletion procedures

5. DOCUMENTATION
- Maintain records of consumer requests and responses
- Document verification methods
- Keep copies of privacy notices
- Record training activities

===== CCPA VS. GDPR KEY DIFFERENCES =====

Scope:
- CCPA: California residents
- GDPR: EU residents

Personal Information:
- CCPA: Broadly defined, includes household data
- GDPR: More specific definition

Rights:
- CCPA: Know, delete, correct, opt-out, limit
- GDPR: Access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, object

Consent:
- CCPA: Opt-out model (except minors)
- GDPR: Opt-in model required for most processing

Enforcement:
- CCPA: State agency + private right of action for breaches
- GDPR: Supervisory authorities + higher fines

Data Protection Officer:
- CCPA: Not required
- GDPR: Required in certain circumstances
